    "# Hypothesis Testing\n",
    "\n",
    "Hypothesis testing is a statistical framework for making decisions from data under uncertainty. We define a **null hypothesis** (H‚ÇÄ) representing the status quo, and an **alternative hypothesis** (H‚ÇÅ) representing the claim we want to support. A test statistic is computed from sample data and compared against a critical value or converted to a p-value.\n",
    "\n",
    "**Key concepts:**\n",
    "- **p-value**: probability of observing data at least as extreme as the sample, assuming H‚ÇÄ is true\n",
    "- **Significance level (Œ±)**: threshold below which we reject H‚ÇÄ (commonly 0.05)\n",
    "- **Type I error**: rejecting a true H‚ÇÄ (false positive, probability = Œ±)\n",
    "- **Type II error**: failing to reject a false H‚ÇÄ (false negative, probability = Œ≤)\n",
    "- **Power**: probability of correctly rejecting a false H‚ÇÄ (= 1 ‚àí Œ≤)\n",
    "\n",
    "This notebook covers the most common parametric and non-parametric tests used in statistics and data science."
   ]

    "## 1. One-Sample t-Test\n",
    "\n",
    "The **one-sample t-test** asks: is the mean of a population equal to a hypothesised value Œº‚ÇÄ?\n",
    "\n",
    "$$H_0: \\mu = \\mu_0 \\qquad H_1: \\mu \\neq \\mu_0$$\n",
    "\n",
    "The test statistic is:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$t = \\frac{\\bar{x} - \\mu_0}{s / \\sqrt{n}}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "where $\\bar{x}$ is the sample mean, $s$ is the sample standard deviation, and $n$ is the sample size. Under H‚ÇÄ this follows a t-distribution with $n - 1$ degrees of freedom.\n",
    "\n",
    "**Assumptions:** observations are independent and (approximately) normally distributed ‚Äî the test is robust to mild departures when $n \\geq 30$."
   ]
  },
  {
   "cell_type": "code",
   "execution_count": null,
   "id": "a1b2c3d4-0004-4000-8000-000000000004",
   "metadata": {},
   "outputs": [],
   "source": [
    "# Scenario: a factory claims its bolts have mean diameter 10 mm.\n",
    "# We sample 40 bolts and want to test that claim.\n",
    "\n",
    "mu_claimed = 10.0          # hypothesised population mean (mm)\n",
    "n = 40\n",
    "true_mean = 10.3           # the factory is actually producing slightly larger bolts\n",
    "true_std  = 0.8\n",
    "\n",
    "sample = rng.normal(loc=true_mean, scale=true_std, size=n)\n",
    "\n",
    "t_stat, p_value = stats.ttest_1samp(sample, popmean=mu_claimed)\n",
    "\n",
    "print(f'Sample mean : {sample.mean():.4f} mm')\n",
    "print(f'Sample std  : {sample.std(ddof=1):.4f} mm')\n",
    "print(f't-statistic : {t_stat:.4f}')\n",
    "print(f'p-value     : {p_value:.4f}')\n",
    "\n",
    "alpha = 0.05\n",
    "if p_value < alpha:\n",
    "    print(f'\\nDecision: Reject H‚ÇÄ (p={p_value:.4f} < Œ±={alpha})')\n",
    "    print(f'The sample mean differs significantly from {mu_claimed} mm.')\n",
    "else:\n",
    "    print(f'\\nDecision: Fail to reject H‚ÇÄ (p={p_value:.4f} ‚â• Œ±={alpha})')\n",
    "    print(f'No significant evidence that the mean differs from {mu_claimed} mm.')\n",
    "\n",
    "# Visualise: sample distribution vs hypothesised mean\n",
    "fig, ax = plt.subplots(figsize=(7, 4))\n",
    "ax.hist(sample, bins=10, color='steelblue', edgecolor='white', alpha=0.8, label='Sample')\n",
    "ax.axvline(sample.mean(), color='steelblue', lw=2, ls='--', label=f'Sample mean ({sample.mean():.2f})')\n",
    "ax.axvline(mu_claimed,    color='tomato',   lw=2, ls='--', label=f'Claimed mean ({mu_claimed})')\n",
    "ax.set_xlabel('Bolt diameter (mm)')\n",
    "ax.set_ylabel('Count')\n",
    "ax.set_title(f'One-sample t-test  |  t={t_stat:.3f}, p={p_value:.4f}')\n",
    "ax.legend()\n",
    "plt.tight_layout()\n",
    "plt.show()"

    "## 2. Two-Sample t-Test (Independent Groups)\n",
    "\n",
    "The **independent two-sample t-test** compares the means of two separate groups:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$H_0: \\mu_1 = \\mu_2 \\qquad H_1: \\mu_1 \\neq \\mu_2$$\n",
    "\n",
    "SciPy's `ttest_ind` uses Welch's t-test by default (`equal_var=False`), which does **not** assume equal variances and is generally preferred over Student's t-test:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$t = \\frac{\\bar{x}_1 - \\bar{x}_2}{\\sqrt{\\frac{s_1^2}{n_1} + \\frac{s_2^2}{n_2}}}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "**When to use:** comparing outcomes between two independent groups (e.g., A/B test, treatment vs control)."
   ]

    "## 3. Paired t-Test\n",
    "\n",
    "The **paired t-test** is used when each observation in one group is matched with exactly one observation in the other group. Common examples: measuring the same subject before and after treatment, or comparing two sensors on the same specimen.\n",
    "\n",
    "$$H_0: \\mu_d = 0 \\qquad H_1: \\mu_d \\neq 0$$\n",
    "\n",
    "where $d_i = x_{i,\\text{after}} - x_{i,\\text{before}}$. This reduces to a one-sample t-test on the differences:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$t = \\frac{\\bar{d}}{s_d / \\sqrt{n}}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "**Advantage over two-sample t-test:** pairing removes between-subject variability, increasing statistical power."
   ]

    "## 4. Chi-Squared Test for Independence\n",
    "\n",
    "The **œá¬≤ test for independence** determines whether two categorical variables are associated. Given an observed contingency table with counts $O_{ij}$ and expected counts $E_{ij}$ under independence:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$\\chi^2 = \\sum_{i,j} \\frac{(O_{ij} - E_{ij})^2}{E_{ij}}, \\qquad E_{ij} = \\frac{R_i \\cdot C_j}{N}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "Degrees of freedom: $(r - 1)(c - 1)$ where $r$ = rows, $c$ = columns.\n",
    "\n",
    "**Assumptions:**\n",
    "- Observations are independent\n",
    "- Expected cell counts ‚â• 5 (use Fisher's exact test when this fails)\n",
    "\n",
    "**When to use:** testing association between categorical variables (e.g., survey responses vs demographic group)."
   ]
  },

    "# Scenario: Is preference for a product (A/B/C) independent of age group (18-34 / 35-54 / 55+)?\n",
    "observed = np.array([\n",
    "    #  Prod A  Prod B  Prod C\n",
    "    [   45,    30,    25],   # 18-34\n",
    "    [   35,    50,    15],   # 35-54\n",
    "    [   20,    20,    60],   # 55+\n",
    "])\n",
    "\n",
    "chi2, p_value, dof, expected = stats.chi2_contingency(observed)\n",
    "\n",
    "print('Observed frequencies:')\n",
    "print(observed)\n",
    "print('\\nExpected frequencies (under independence):')\n",
    "print(np.round(expected, 1))\n",
    "print(f'\\nœá¬≤ statistic : {chi2:.4f}')\n",
    "print(f'Degrees of freedom: {dof}')\n",
    "print(f'p-value      : {p_value:.6f}')\n",
    "\n",
    "alpha = 0.05\n",
    "if p_value < alpha:\n",
    "    print(f'\\nDecision: Reject H‚ÇÄ ‚Äî product preference IS associated with age group.')\n",
    "else:\n",
    "    print(f'\\nDecision: Fail to reject H‚ÇÄ ‚Äî no significant association detected.')\n",
    "\n",
    "# Heatmap of standardised residuals\n",
    "std_residuals = (observed - expected) / np.sqrt(expected)\n",
    "\n",
    "fig, axes = plt.subplots(1, 2, figsize=(11, 4))\n",

    "\n",
    "for ax, data, title, fmt in zip(\n",
    "        axes,\n",

    "        ['Observed counts', 'Standardised residuals  (>|2| = notable)'],\n",
    "        ['.0f', '.2f']):\n",
    "    im = ax.imshow(data, cmap='RdYlGn' if 'residual' in title.lower() else 'Blues',\n",
    "                   aspect='auto')\n",
    "    ax.set_xticks(range(3)); ax.set_xticklabels(product_labels)\n",
    "    ax.set_yticks(range(3)); ax.set_yticklabels(age_labels)\n",

    "        for j in range(3):\n",

    "    ax.set_title(title)\n",
    "    plt.colorbar(im, ax=ax, shrink=0.8)\n",
    "\n",
    "plt.suptitle(f'Chi-squared test  |  œá¬≤={chi2:.2f}, df={dof}, p={p_value:.4f}', y=1.02)\n",

    "## 5. One-Way ANOVA\n",
    "\n",
    "**Analysis of Variance (ANOVA)** tests whether the means of three or more independent groups are all equal:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$H_0: \\mu_1 = \\mu_2 = \\cdots = \\mu_k \\qquad H_1: \\text{at least one } \\mu_i \\neq \\mu_j$$\n",
    "\n",
    "The F-statistic partitions total variance into:\n",
    "- **Between-group variance** (signal): variation explained by group membership\n",
    "- **Within-group variance** (noise): variation within each group\n",
    "\n",
    "$$F = \\frac{\\text{MS}_{\\text{between}}}{\\text{MS}_{\\text{within}}}$$\n",
    "\n",
    "**Assumptions:** independence, normality within groups, homogeneity of variances (Levene's test).\n",
    "\n",
    "A significant ANOVA result only tells us *some* means differ ‚Äî post-hoc tests (Tukey HSD, Bonferroni) identify *which* pairs."
   ]
  },
  {
   "cell_type": "code",
   "execution_count": null,
   "id": "a1b2c3d4-0012-4000-8000-000000000012",
   "metadata": {},
   "outputs": [],
   "source": [
    "# Scenario: compare crop yield (kg/plot) under three fertiliser treatments.\n",
    "n_per_group = 20\n",
    "control  = rng.normal(loc=50, scale=8, size=n_per_group)\n",
    "fert_A   = rng.normal(loc=55, scale=8, size=n_per_group)\n",
    "fert_B   = rng.normal(loc=62, scale=9, size=n_per_group)\n",
    "\n",
    "f_stat, p_value = stats.f_oneway(control, fert_A, fert_B)\n",
    "\n",

    "## 6. Multiple Testing Correction\n",
    "\n",
    "When we perform $m$ hypothesis tests simultaneously, the probability of at least one false positive increases rapidly. If each test has type I error rate Œ±, the **family-wise error rate (FWER)** is:\n",
    "\n",
    "$$\\text{FWER} = 1 - (1 - \\alpha)^m$$\n",
    "\n",
    "For $m = 20$ tests at Œ± = 0.05: FWER ‚âà 64%!\n",
    "\n",
    "**Bonferroni correction** is the simplest method: adjust the threshold to $\\alpha^* = \\alpha / m$, or equivalently multiply each p-value by $m$ (capping at 1).\n",
    "\n",
    "**Benjamini-Hochberg (BH)** controls the **False Discovery Rate (FDR)** instead ‚Äî less conservative and more powerful when many tests are conducted (e.g., genomics, neuroimaging).\n",
    "\n",
    "| Method | Controls | Conservative? | Use when |\n",
    "|--------|----------|---------------|---------|\n",
    "| Bonferroni | FWER | Very | Few tests, confirmatory study |\n",
    "| Holm-Bonferroni | FWER | Moderately | Few tests, step-down |\n",
    "| Benjamini-Hochberg | FDR | Mildly | Many tests, exploratory study |"

    "raw_pvals = np.array([stats.ttest_1samp(s, popmean=0).pvalue for s in all_samples])\n",
    "\n",
    "# Bonferroni correction\n",
    "bonferroni_pvals = np.minimum(raw_pvals * m_total, 1.0)\n",
    "\n",
    "# Benjamini-Hochberg (manual implementation)\n",
    "def bh_correction(pvals, fdr=0.05):\n",
    "    n = len(pvals)\n",
    "    sorted_idx = np.argsort(pvals)\n",
    "    sorted_p = pvals[sorted_idx]\n",
    "    thresholds = fdr * np.arange(1, n + 1) / n\n",
    "    reject_mask = sorted_p <= thresholds\n",
    "    # Find the largest k such that p_(k) <= k/m * FDR\n",
    "    if reject_mask.any():\n",
    "        cutoff = np.where(reject_mask)[0].max()\n",
    "        final_reject = np.zeros(n, dtype=bool)\n",
    "        final_reject[sorted_idx[:cutoff + 1]] = True\n",
    "    else:\n",
    "        final_reject = np.zeros(n, dtype=bool)\n",
    "    return final_reject\n",
